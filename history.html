HISTORY OF COMPOSITION STUDIES

History of Composition Studies explored multiple narratives framing the growth and trajectory of this academic field. Providing a top-down history, this class investigates various trends and methodologies that ground composition as an academic study.

"AAPI VOICES IN COMPOSITION: COMPARATIVE RHETORIC AS WASIAN REPRESENTATION"

“AAPI Voices in Composition: Comparative Rhetoric as Wasian Representation” is a report that summarizes my perception of composition and rhetoric as someone—a queer, mixed-Asian woman—who represents a population that has been historically excluded from academia. I define comparative rhetoric and investigate multimedia literacy narratives as methods to give voice to underrepresented communities, including my own.

This essay allowed me to synthesize important histories in composition with own perspective operating in academia. In this project, I engaged with comparative rhetoric as a framework to examine how marginalized voices—particularly those of queer, mixed-Asian individuals—have been excluded from and might be introduced into composition studies. By analyzing multimedia literacy narratives, I demonstrated how alternative forms of storytelling can expand what counts as valid knowledge and participation in the field. This work reflects my ability to connect historical and theoretical frameworks to lived experience, highlighting how identity informs my approach to research and writing. Ultimately, the project underscores my growing understanding of composition as an evolving discipline and my commitment to contributing more inclusive perspectives in my future.
