CONTENT MANAGEMENT

Web Content Management focused on the theory and practice of content management and strategy in professional and organizational settings. Here, I explored how digital content is created, structured, governed, and evaluated across platforms to support organizational goals and user needs. Emphasis is placed on rhetorical decision-making, audience analysis, content strategy frameworks, and the use of content management systems (CMS).

"WEB CONTENT MANAGEMENT SYSTEMS"

The infographic “Web Content Management Systems for Small Businesses & Start-ups” condenses complex information about CMS for a general audience of emerging small business owners. I outline three popular CMS and outline notable features of each: Shopify, Square, and Adobe Commerce.

This infographic allowed me to translate complex information about CMS into clear, accessible information both in writing and visual form. In developing this project, I applied content strategy principles such as audience analysis, rhetorical decision-making, and structured organization to present the features of Shopify, Square, and Adobe Commerce in a way that is accessible to non-expert users. This work demonstrates that content must be curated and adapted to meet both user needs and organizational goals, therefore reinforcing my ability to design concise, informative materials. By connecting theory to practice, this assignment highlights my growing competence in content strategy and my ability to create purposeful, user-focused communication.
