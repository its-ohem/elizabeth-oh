INFORMATION DESIGN

Information Design focused on theories and design of documentation for computer software, including user guides, video tutorials, and online documentation. I learned principles of documentations that are applicable across different contexts of technical communication practice such as multimedia technical communication, structured writing, and content audits. Particularly, I learned to draft content in DITA, a digital architecture which organizes content into various document types.

"TRANSLATING INSTRUCTIONS INTO DITA"

The video “Translating Instructions into DITA” presents an educational infographic that outlines a structured approach to transforming instructional content into three DITA content types (task, concept, and reference). By using accessible language, I teach an audience of hypothetical instructors to generate student email lists from Canvas, an online educational platform.

“Translating Instructions into DITA” enabled me to demonstrate my understanding of DITA by organizing information into task, concept, and reference types in order to make technical processes accessible across technical communication contexts. This educational infographic and explanation, delivered in a web conference-style video, allowed me to practice sharing complex information about both educational processes and DITA documentation. This project reflects my ability to create effective, adaptable documentation and present the ways in which structured content can support technical communication and user-focused content development.
