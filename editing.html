Editing Professional Documents focused on preparing content for publication or correspondence, including memos, instructional manuals, articles, and websites. I developed style guides and learned to provide high- and low-level feedback on multimedia products.

"A QUICKSTART GUIDE TO COPYEDITING FOR THE ENGLISH STUDENT"

The article “A Quickstart Guide to Copyediting for the English Student” imitates a blog post while summarizing key lessons about copyediting for a specific audience: English college students. It references The Subversive Copy Editor to provide credibility and incorporates various forms of media, including photos and bullet lists, to maximize accessibility and SEO performance.

This article demonstrates my ability to produce polished, audience-centered content that balances clarity, correctness, and engagement—all important qualities of an editor. In this project, I adapted my knowledge of style guides and editing practices to create a blog-style resource tailored specifically to English college students, using accessible language and organization of content to enhance readability. By incorporating insights from The Subversive Copy Editor, I grounded my work in established editorial theory while also demonstrating my ability to translate professional standards into practical advice. Additionally, my use of multimedia elements such as images and bullet lists reflects my understanding of graphic design to support accessibility and SEO performance in digital environments. This project highlights my growth in editing skills and ability to produce publication-ready content that aligns with professional communication standards.
