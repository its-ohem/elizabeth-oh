COMMUNICATIONS & STORYTELLING INTERNSHIP

As part of the curriculum for the Professional Writing master’s program, I worked as a Communications & Storytelling Intern at the leadership consulting organization M E Clarke Consulting, LLC. This position allowed me to apply the rhetorical strategies and scholarship I’d learned in a real-world writing job, a valuable opportunity which bridged the gap between theory and work.

"INTERNSHIP REPORT"

This Internship Report is a thorough compilation of all the materials I produced for the organization, particularly blog posts and flyers. Accompanying each product is a rhetorical analysis, linking the skills I learned in the classroom to responsibilities of the internship. I also provide recommendations for future Professional Writing students who seek internships as well as a personal reflection of my experience.

This report represents the culmination of my education within a tangible job, applying skills I’d been developing my whole college career. The purpose of this report is to document and reflect on the range of materials I produced while analyzing how each piece was shaped by audience, organizational goals, and strategic communication practices. In my role, I was responsible for crafting content that aligned with the company’s leadership-focused messaging, requiring me to adapt my writing style to meet internal expectations and external audience needs. Including this work in my portfolio highlights my ability to bridge academic knowledge with workplace application, particularly through my use of rhetorical analysis to reflect on and evaluate the effectiveness of own work.
