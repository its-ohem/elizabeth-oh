Introduction to Professional Writing

Introduction to Professional Writing focused on professional writing as an academic discipline and profession. I tracked the rise of professional writing with a focus on key theoretical issues, research methods, and career options. This course acted as both seminar and practicum, challenging me with multimedia writing tasks to probe the historical and theoretical issues of professional writing.

"UNIVERSAL DESIGN IN THE SIGNAGE OF JAPAN: CASE STUDY OF VISUAL COMMUNICATION ON THE SUBWAY"

“Universal Design in the Signage of Japan: Case Study of Visual Communication on the Subway” is a rhetorical analysis of signage I encountered while navigating subway systems in Japan. This document identifies exactly what visual strategies are implemented in these documents to make them effective educational tools, even to non-native passengers.

In this project, I applied foundational concepts in professional writing by observing the effectiveness of signage to convey information to a new user (myself) in an unfamiliar environment (subway systems in a foreign country). I engaged directly with real-world multimedia, photographing signage and analyzing how visual strategies such as iconography, color coding, and spatial organization function to communicate across language barriers. This hands-on approach reinforced the importance of field research in understanding how users actually interact with professional texts in context. Additionally, focusing on user experience helped me recognize how professional writers must anticipate diverse audiences and accessibility needs. By linking theoretical frameworks to lived environments, I developed practical skills in research, analysis, and audience-centered design that are essential for my future work in professional writing.
