FEMINIST METHODS

Feminist Methods focused on methodological and epistemological questions in the field of Women’s, Gender, and Sexuality Studies. I learned a variety of interdisciplinary, feminist research methods and their use in existing literature with an emphasis on qualitative research methods.

"HAPA MIDWEST: PROPOSAL FOR AN OBJECT-ORIENTED AUTOETHNOGRAPHY"

“Hapa Midwest: Proposal for an Object-Oriented Autoethnography” is a research proposal that describes the knowledge gap about queer, mixed Korean-American lives and presents my autoethnography as a method to make up for this overseen population. I define what queerness means as it intersects my ethnic identity and argue autoethnography is the best strategy to identify critical insights about the experiences of queer, mixed Korean-Americans.

This research proposal allowed me to synthesize feminist theory with my own identity by engaging with epistemological questions surrounding identity, representation, and knowledge production. In this proposal, I identified a significant gap in research on queer, mixed Korean-American experiences and argued for the use of autoethnography to center lived experience as a feminist, valid, and necessary form of knowledge. By defining queerness and its intersection with my own ethnic identity, I demonstrated the importance of reflexive, qualitative methods in capturing marginalized perspectives. This project reflects my ability to connect theory to research design while highlighting my contributions to inclusive, accessible, feminist scholarship.
