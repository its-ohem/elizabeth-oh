RESPONSIBLE AI IN TECHNICAL & PROFESSIONAL COMMUNICATION

Responsible AI in Technical & Professional Communication focused on the rhetorical, ethical, and cultural dimensions of AI in technical and professional communication practices. I investigated emerging trends and theories that apply AI to various domains such as writing, recruitment, and business. Particularly, I learned about the principles of algorithm and usability audits to critically evaluate and develop responsible AI applications.

"AI AUDIT: FREEDAI"

The report “AI Audit: FreedAI” follows my audit of the FreedAI medical scribe technology. I assess its ability to draw conclusions from audio transcripts by replicating an encounter I had with my own doctor, then evaluating the quality of its response. I identify strengths and weaknesses of the program before applying ethical theory to this technology, including use cases that risk violating patient confidentiality and exasperating unequal treatment by medical practitioners.

The “AI Audit: FreedAI” report allowed me to apply an ethical perspective to FreedAI as a representative of emerging AI technologies. In auditing FreedAI, I applied principles of algorithmic and usability analysis by replicating a real doctor-patient interaction and assessing how effectively the program generated conclusions from audio transcripts. This hands-on approach demonstrated my ability to test AI tools in realistic contexts while identifying functional strengths and critical limitations. Importantly, I extended this evaluation by incorporating ethical frameworks and analyzing the risk of AI bias medical contexts. This project reflects my ability to connect theoretical discussions of AI to real-world applications, highlighting my preparedness to engage responsibly with emerging technologies in professional communication contexts.
